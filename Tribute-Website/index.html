Ratan Naval Tata

1937-2025

The Visionary Industrialist of India

Ratan Naval Tata (born 28 December 1937) is an Indian industrialist, philanthropist, and former chairman of Tata Sons and the Tata Group, serving from 1990 to 2012 and again as interim chairman from October 2016 to January 2017. He is widely recognized for transforming the Tata Group from a nationally focused company into a global business conglomerate by acquiring major international brands such as Jaguar Land Rover, Tetley Tea, and Corus Steel. Under his leadership, the Tata Group expanded into more than 100 countries across six continents, with revenue increasing more than 40 times. He also introduced India’s most affordable car, the Tata Nano, fulfilling his vision of safe and economic transportation for middle-class families. Beyond business, Ratan Tata is respected for his simplicity, humility, ethics, and commitment to social welfare. Through the Tata Trusts, he has contributed billions to education, healthcare, rural development, cancer research, and technology innovation. His legacy continues to inspire young entrepreneurs across the world.
